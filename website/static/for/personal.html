For individuals

Recover your own iPhone data

Your messages, your photos, your contacts, your voicemails — they're all sitting inside your iPhone backup right now. OpenExtract gets them out, for free, without uploading anything to a cloud.

If you've ever tried to pull a specific text thread off your phone, export your photos with their dates intact, or find a voicemail from someone you miss, you know the official Apple tools don't really let you do it. iCloud is a backup, not an archive you can read. Your iPhone backup on your computer is a pile of files you can't open in any normal way.

OpenExtract opens it.

What you can get out of a backup

  • Text messages — the full thread, with dates, in a readable format. Not screenshots.
  • Photos and videos — including their original dates, locations, and camera info.
  • Contacts — names, numbers, emails, notes, birthdays, everything.
  • Call history — who, when, how long.
  • Voicemails — the actual audio files.
  • Notes and calendars.
  • Messages from WhatsApp, Signal, Telegram, and other apps.
  • Any thread you deleted from your phone after the backup was made — it's still in that backup, frozen in time.

If you made your iPhone backup with a password (encrypted backup — recommended), OpenExtract gets even more: health data, saved Wi-Fi networks, browser history.

What OpenExtract does not do: forensic recovery of anything that isn't in the backup. It reads the backup you already have. If a thread was deleted before the backup was made, every backup on disk already reflects that deletion — no tool that reads backups can bring it back.

Common reasons people use it

"I want to save the messages from someone important to me."
A family member, a partner, a friend. You want the thread, saved as a PDF or printed, not stuck inside an iPhone forever.

"I'm switching to Android and I want my stuff."
Contacts, photos, messages — all of it. OpenExtract exports to formats you can import into other tools or keep as an archive.

"I accidentally deleted a text and I need it back."
If you backed up your phone before the deletion, the thread is still in that backup — OpenExtract opens it and gives you the full conversation back as a PDF or HTML file. If the deletion happened before your most recent backup, though, there's nothing on your computer that still has those messages, and no tool (free or paid) can recover them from the backups you have.

"I just want my photos with their real dates."
Apple's Photos app renames and reorganizes. OpenExtract extracts photos and videos with their original filenames, dates, and GPS locations intact.

"Someone I love has passed away and I want to preserve their messages."
With authorization, OpenExtract can read the backup of a decedent's iPhone (which you'd typically have access to via Apple's digital legacy process or as the estate representative). We've heard from people who used it to preserve final conversations with parents, siblings, and spouses.

"I'm getting divorced and my lawyer asked for my text messages."
OpenExtract's PDF exports preserve timestamps and are formatted for legal use. See OpenExtract for legal professionals for details.

Is this safe?

Yes. Here's what you can verify:

  • Your data never leaves your computer. OpenExtract makes no internet connections to do its job. You can confirm this with any network monitor.
  • The code is open source. Everyone can read it. Anyone who wants to audit it can. The full source is at github.com/charleswest775/openextract.
  • The installers are signed (Authenticode on Windows, Developer ID on macOS). Your operating system verifies authenticity before the app runs.
  • No account, no sign-in, no data collection. There's nothing to sign up for.

This is different from an app that promises privacy. It's an app you can prove is private, because the code is there for anyone to check.

How to use it

  1. Back up your iPhone to your computer. Plug it into your Mac or PC and back up through Finder (macOS) or the Apple Devices / iTunes app (Windows). Choose "Encrypt local backup" and set a password — encrypted backups contain more data and are what OpenExtract works best with.
  2. Download OpenExtract. Free, for Windows, macOS, or Linux.
  3. Open OpenExtract and point it at your backup. The app finds the standard location automatically. If your backup is encrypted, enter the password.
  4. Pick what you want. Specific contacts? A date range? All photos? Every voicemail? Choose.
  5. Export it. PDF, CSV, HTML, or original files. Save anywhere on your computer.

Most first-time users are done in under ten minutes.

Honest about what it doesn't do

  • If you've never backed up your iPhone to your computer, there's nothing to extract. iCloud backups aren't the same thing — they're encrypted on Apple's servers and not readable by any third-party tool. Make a local backup first.
  • If you've lost your iPhone backup password, OpenExtract can't recover it. Apple can't either — that's the security model. Password-recovery tools exist but are a separate problem.
  • If you don't have the physical device or an existing backup, OpenExtract has nothing to work with. It's not a hacking tool. It's a tool for your own backups.
  • Your iPhone has to have been unlocked to create the backup in the first place. OpenExtract doesn't bypass passcodes.

It's free. Really.

There's no trial, no "free to try, $29.99 to unlock exports." No account to create. No email address to give up. No upsell screens. You download it, you use it, it works, you're done. If we wanted to collect your data, we'd need servers — and we don't have any.
